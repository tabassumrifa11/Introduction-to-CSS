Bangladesh Gas Fields Company Limited

Bangladesh Gas Fields Company Limited, abbreviated as BGFCL, is a major gas fields company that the Bangladesh government owns through Petrobangla.

The BGFCL owns---

  • Titash Gas Field
  • Habiganj Gas Field
  • Bakhrabad Gas Field
  • Narsingdi Gas Field
  • Bakhrabad Gas Field
  • Meghna Gas Field
  • Kamta Gas Field
  • Sangu Gas Field

Bangladesh Gas Fields Company Limited (BGFCL) is a state-owned enterprise responsible for the exploration, production, and management of natural gas resources in Bangladesh. Established on September 12, 1975, BGFCL traces its origins to the Pakistan Shell Oil Company, which was formed in Karachi on May 30, 1956. Following Bangladesh's independence, the government acquired all shares of the Pakistan Shell Oil Company on August 9, 1975, leading to the formation of BGFCL

BGFCL's corporate office is located in Brahmanbaria, Chittagong Division, with a liaison office at Petro Centre (14th Floor), 3, Karwan Bazar C.A., Dhaka-1212. The company employs a significant workforce dedicated to managing and operating these gas fields, ensuring a steady supply of natural gas to meet the country's energy needs.
